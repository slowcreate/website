OUR STORY

Opened in 2014, Ark Books is a nonprofit shop offering a diverse collection of world literature, primerely in English. However we also feature books in German, French, and Spanish. With a focus on translations, we curate multiple versions of some titles. Our goal is to showcase both familiar and undiscovered literature from across the globe. Run by volunteers within an open and democratic association, our earnings cover rent, new books, events, and maintain low prices. We're a community-driven space, striving for inclusivity where everyone can freely enjoy books, discussions, and complimentary coffee or tea. Your presence is always warmly welcomed.

In 2011, the idea for ark books emerged over red wine and candlelight in Copenhagen. Two years later, a small room became available, triggering plans to create an international bookshop. Seeking funding proved challenging, as traditional avenues didn't fit. Instead, we turned to crowdfunding, rallying over 300 supporters to surpass our goal. With donations, loans, and personal savings, ark books took shape. Intensive preparations ensued, culminating in a whirlwind 20-day sprint to transform a tiny space into a bookshop. As the doors opened, unexpected newspaper coverage added to the surreal moment. The grand opening evolved into a lively street celebration.

And that's the story of how a community-centered bookshop found its home in a small space on Møllegade 10, born from passion, determination, and support from around the world.

Contributors

All the people who have contributed to Ark

Sarah Ommanney

Sarah, former child prodigy, has now shed the vanities of youth by shaving off her eyebrows, yet continues to impress. She has co-authored several books with Giovanna Alesandro and regularly shocks the artworld with her world-renowned cartoons.

Rebecca Lund

Rebecca is a feminist academic and activist. She has far too many projects for her own good; she leaves a massive carbon footprint annually; and is always searching for excuses to rest under a tree with a novel and a glass of wine. She misses the Finnish forests; would like to visit Cinemateket more often and ought to exercise more regularly. She has far to many projects for her own good and can be found by following a trail of half finished cups of coffee and/or tea.

Alexander Buk-Swienty

Alexander happily holds a Master’s degree in Comparative Literature from the University of Copenhagen. He currently works for a literary agency where he spends his time staring out the window and writing e-mails to obscure publishers in Eastern Europe. He is ostensibly a volunteer at Ark Books, but no one can remember the last time he took a shift. For the Ark Review he writes various analysis of literary things with Lacan as the theoretical spearhead. A deceivingly brilliant field to pick of course, because no one understands Lacan, and thus Alexander comes off as smart. He asks for all complaints or disagreements concerning his articles be addressed to the big Other.

Emilie Bryde Bang-Jensen

Aspiring writer and avid reader of fiction. Has an odd penchant for white, American male authors such as Don DeLillo, David Foster Wallace, and Jonathan Franzen. Likes to discuss the failings of neoliberalism and other systems of oppression. Has yet to find a way to do anything about them. Had her eyes opened by postcolonial and gender theory (which has yet to do anything to her love of aforementioned white American male authors). Prefers Nescafé over real coffee, which everyone in the bookshop finds strange.
